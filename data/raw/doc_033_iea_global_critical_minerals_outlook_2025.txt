SUMMARY:
IEA Global Critical Minerals Outlook 2025 examines mineral supply, demand, concentration, investment, recycling, and processing for energy technologies. It is relevant because semiconductors, batteries, grids, electric vehicles, defense systems, and renewable energy all depend on specialized materials. The source helps a RAG assistant connect upstream minerals to downstream technology resilience. It is useful for distinguishing mining concentration, refining concentration, geopolitical exposure, price volatility, and substitution limits.

USEFUL_PARAPHRASED_TEXT_FOR_INGESTION:
This source should be used when technology risk depends on minerals rather than finished products alone. A RAG assistant should distinguish where concentration occurs: extraction, processing, refining, component manufacturing, or final assembly. A country may mine a mineral but depend on another country for processing.
The report is useful for linking energy transition, industrial policy, and national security. Lithium, nickel, cobalt, graphite, rare earth elements, copper, and other materials affect batteries, motors, grid equipment, electronics, and defense-adjacent systems. Supply disruption can arise from geopolitics, permitting delays, underinvestment, environmental constraints, or export controls.
For contradiction testing, pair this source with USGS summaries, DOE assessments, and semiconductor material-risk reports. Human review should trigger for investment, procurement, or sanctions-sensitive conclusions.
