SUMMARY:
This RAND report analyzes how the Russia-Ukraine war has affected U.S. and European defense industrial bases. It examines prewar conditions, wartime challenges, procurement, production capacity, workforce issues, and allied coordination. RAND finds that reforms have been positive but modest and that key production lines still face challenges. It argues that sustained funding and multiyear procurement are important for modernization and production capacity. The report is useful because it links battlefield consumption to industrial capacity and future conflict readiness. It also provides a non-governmental assessment that can be compared with DoD strategy and GAO oversight.

USEFUL_PARAPHRASED_TEXT_FOR_INGESTION:
This source examines the defense industrial lessons of the Russia-Ukraine war. Its core value is that it connects battlefield demand to production systems. The war demonstrated that modern conflict can consume large quantities of munitions, drones, air-defense interceptors, artillery, vehicles, spare parts, and electronic systems. If the industrial base cannot replenish stocks or scale production, military support and deterrence are constrained.

RAND’s analysis is useful because it is more evaluative than a policy announcement. It recognizes reforms in the United States and Europe but describes them as modest relative to the scale of the challenge. It highlights persistent constraints such as production capacity, workforce availability, procurement practices, funding uncertainty, supply-chain vulnerabilities, and coordination problems. It also notes that investment in Ukraine’s own defense industrial base may become strategically important for Europe.
