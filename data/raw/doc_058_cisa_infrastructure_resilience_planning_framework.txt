SUMMARY:
CISA Infrastructure Resilience Planning Framework helps communities and organizations understand dependencies, prioritize infrastructure, assess risks, and plan resilience improvements. It is relevant because many OSINT sources describe threats but not community-level consequence analysis. The source helps the assistant connect assets, hazards, dependencies, owners, operators, lifeline services, and recovery priorities. It pairs well with energy, water, cyber, and disaster-response sources.

WHY_USEFUL_FOR_RAG_OSINT_DEMO:
Useful for turning threat evidence into dependency and consequence analysis for infrastructure resilience.

USEFUL_PARAPHRASED_TEXT_FOR_INGESTION:
This source should be used when the query asks what disruption would mean for a place or community. A RAG assistant should map assets, dependencies, lifeline functions, owners, operators, hazards, and recovery priorities.
The framework is useful for distinguishing criticality from visibility. A small substation, bridge, water pump, hospital supplier, or communications node may be more consequential than a more visible asset if many services depend on it.
For contradiction testing, pair this source with cyber and energy reports. Human review should trigger for emergency planning, public safety, or asset-specific vulnerability discussion.
