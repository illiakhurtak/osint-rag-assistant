SUMMARY:
NIST AI Risk Management Framework provides a voluntary structure for mapping, measuring, managing, and governing AI risks. It is useful for an OSINT RAG assistant because RAG systems can produce hallucinations, misattribute claims, expose sensitive information, amplify misinformation, or overstate confidence. The framework helps classify AI risks by validity, reliability, safety, security, privacy, explainability, fairness, and accountability. It also supports evaluation design for human review and confidence labeling.

WHY_USEFUL_FOR_RAG_OSINT_DEMO:
Useful for evaluating the RAG assistant itself, especially uncertainty, citations, and safety flags.

USEFUL_PARAPHRASED_TEXT_FOR_INGESTION:
This source should be used as a governance reference for AI system behavior. A RAG assistant has risks that differ from ordinary search: it can combine sources incorrectly, cite irrelevant passages, hide uncertainty, or produce polished but unsupported claims. The framework helps express those risks in a structured way.
For OSINT use, important concepts include provenance, measurement, monitoring, human oversight, privacy, robustness, and explainability. The assistant should state when evidence is incomplete and should avoid creating certainty from weak retrieval. It should also preserve distinctions between allegation, analysis, official confirmation, and observed data.
For contradiction testing, pair this source with OECD disinformation guidance and local evaluation results. Human review should trigger when retrieved evidence involves public safety, conflict, elections, sanctions, named individuals, or operational security.
